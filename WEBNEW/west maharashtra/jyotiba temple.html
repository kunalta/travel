Jyotiba Temple is a holy site of Hinduism near Wadi Ratnagiri [1] in Kolhapur district of Maharashtra state in western India. The deity of the temple is known by the same name, and is held by the locals to be an incarnation of three gods: Brahma, Vishnu, Mahesha, and Jamadagni.[2] An annual fair takes place on the full moon night of the Hindu months of Chaitra and Vaishakha.
